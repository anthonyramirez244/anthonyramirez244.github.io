About

I'm an AI engineer and full-stack developer who builds autonomous systems from the ground up — from low-level audio pipelines and system-capture overlays to cloud-native DevOps agents and MCP server APIs.

My approach is local-first and practical: real tools that solve real problems. When I built Halo, I reverse-engineered commercial software, figured out why WASAPI loopback misses WebRTC audio, and wired up a VB-Cable bridge instead. When off-the-shelf agents weren't good enough, I built a custom slash-command ecosystem of 11 named AI personas.

I'm drawn to the intersection of AI and systems programming — getting models to interact with hardware, audio streams, screen capture, and real-time APIs in ways that actually hold up under production conditions.
